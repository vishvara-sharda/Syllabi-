Module 1: Foundations of UX & Behavioral Science
Week 1–4

Learning Objectives: Understand the role of UX research and human factors, and build groundwork in cognitive psychology. Learn formal terminology, research ethics, and problem framing.

Week 1 – Introduction & Foundations: Advanced UX research role, mixed methods overview, project planning, ethics. Introduction to behavioral science in UX. UX research ethics and privacy. Defining problem statements and research goals.
Key Readings/Resources: Dovetail “UX Researcher Roles”; NN/g “Psychology for UX” (Intro, Attention); Fogg (2009) Behavior Model; NN/g on UX ethics.
Week 2 – User-Centered Design & Cognitive Psychology: Gestalt laws, mental models, cognitive load, memory, attention limits, bias. Nielsen’s usability principles.
Key Readings/Resources: Don Norman, The Design of Everyday Things; NN/g articles on Gestalt principles and memory; IDF cognitive bias short course.
Week 3 – Cognitive Biases: Framing, confirmation, anchoring, decision frames, prospect theory, mental models.
Key Readings/Resources: NN/g “Decision Frames”; psychology-for-UX readings.
Week 4 – Motivation & Behavior Models: Motivation, emotion, and behavior-change theory. Fogg Behavior Model, COM-B, Self-Determination.
Key Readings/Resources: IxDF “Persuasive Design”; Fogg’s Behavior Model.

Assignments & Activities: Draft a research goal statement, analyze a UI using Gestalt principles, evaluate a study result through bias framing, and identify one app element explained by Fogg’s model.

Deliverables: Written research goal, UI analysis notes, bias-case analysis.

Module 2: Qualitative UX Research Methods
Week 5–8

Learning Objectives: Master generative and evaluative qualitative methods. Learn to conduct and document interviews, observations, diary studies, and usability tests. Develop coding and synthesis skills.

Week 5 – Interviews & Contextual Inquiry: Interview types, question design, listening skills, contextual inquiry basics.
Key Readings/Resources: UX Field Guide – “User Interviews” (UIQ toolkit); interview chapters from Handbook of Usability Testing.
Week 6 – Observational Methods: Ethnography, field study, diary studies.
Key Readings/Resources: NN/g “Research for Journey Mapping”; UXPA ethnographic studies article.
Week 7 – Usability Testing: Moderated/unmoderated testing, remote vs lab, think-aloud protocol.
Key Readings/Resources: Nielsen Norman “Usability Testing 101”; Paper Prototyping; heuristic evaluation basics.
Week 8 – Data Analysis (Qual): Thematic analysis, affinity diagramming, coding qualitative data.
Key Readings/Resources: UX Field Guide – “Analyzing User Research”; NN/g on research analysis.

Assignments & Activities: Conduct 5 interviews, document observations, run a 2-user usability test, and synthesize themes into personas and a journey map draft.

Deliverables: Interview transcripts, observation notes, affinity diagram output, draft persona & journey.

Module 3: Quantitative Methods & Analytics
Week 9–12

Learning Objectives: Learn survey design and statistical basics. Understand UX metrics, A/B testing, analytics, and how to derive insights from quantitative data.

Week 9 – Surveys & Questionnaires: Likert scales, question types, sampling, online survey tools.
Key Readings/Resources: UX Field Guide – “Surveys”; Google Forms; Qualtrics; SurveyMonkey.
Week 10 – Web Analytics & Metrics: Page views, time on page, bounce rate, funnels, heatmaps.
Key Readings/Resources: NN/g “Evidence-based Interaction Design”; Google Analytics; Hotjar.
Week 11 – A/B and Experimental Testing: Hypothesis setting, significance, test design.
Key Readings/Resources: UX Field Guide – “A/B Testing”; Optimizely best-practice resources.
Week 12 – Quantitative Analysis: Mean, median, mode, standard deviation, charting results.
Key Readings/Resources: Statistics for UX; Excel/Sheets charting tutorials.

Assignments & Activities: Design a 10-question survey, analyze analytics data, plan an A/B test, and compute descriptive stats in Excel.

Deliverables: Survey instrument and results report, analytics screenshot, A/B test plan & findings.

Module 4: Data Analysis, Synthesis & Reporting
Week 13–18

Learning Objectives: Turn raw research into themes, insights, personas, journeys, and stakeholder-ready communication.

Week 13 – Cognitive Biases & Decision-Making: Confirmation, choice overload, scarcity, social proof.
Key Readings/Resources: NN/g “Decision Frames”; behavioral design Q&A.
Week 14 – Persuasive & Habit Design: Fogg’s Behavior Model, Hook Model, persuasive design principles.
Key Readings/Resources: IxDF persuasive design; Hooked; Nudge.
Week 15 – Emotion & Behavioral Triggers: Emotional design, gamification, social incentives.
Key Readings/Resources: Norman’s Emotional Design; Behavioral Design by Stephen Wendel.
Week 16 – Ethics of Behavioral UX: Dark patterns vs ethical nudges, user autonomy.
Key Readings/Resources: ethics in persuasive UX resources; Nielsen/Norman ethics material.
Week 17–18 – Reporting & Communication: Storytelling with data, slide decks, written reports, stakeholder communication, presentation structure.
Key Readings/Resources: NN/g presentation guides; Few’s Now You See It; Duarte’s Resonate; GDPR basics.

Assignments & Activities: Synthesize interview and survey findings into personas and journeys, prepare a slide deck, and peer-review report clarity.

Deliverables: Personas PDF, journey map visual, research presentation slides, executive summary document.

Module 5: Design Systems & UI Standards
Week 19–20

Learning Objectives: Understand what design systems are and why they matter. Learn to create and use a design system for consistency and scalability.

Week 19 – Design Systems & UI Patterns: Component libraries, forms, navigation patterns, style guides, design tokens, consistency.
Key Readings/Resources: NN/g “Design Systems 101”; InVision Design System Handbook; Figma component library tutorials.
Week 20 – Building Blocks (Styles & Tokens): Color palettes, typography, grid, components, patterns, documentation.
Key Readings/Resources: Figma design system resources; Storybook; IBM Carbon or Google Material Design docs.

Assignments & Activities: Audit a product for consistency, create a mini style guide, build UI components in Figma, and write usage guidelines for a component.

Deliverables: Mini design system style guide, component library screenshot, documentation snippet.

Module 6: Accessibility, Inclusive Design & Specialized UX
Week 21–22

Learning Objectives: Learn to research with diverse users and ensure accessibility. Explore behavioral economics and specialized UX contexts.

Week 21 – Accessibility & Inclusive Design: WCAG guidelines, screen readers, color contrast, motor/visual/cognitive accessibility, inclusive methods.
Key Readings/Resources: WCAG 2.1 Quick Reference; Inclusive Design for Web; NN/g accessibility usability testing report; A11Y checklist.
Week 22 – Behavioral UX Specialization: Behavioral economics, loss aversion, framing, choice architecture, habit-forming features, dark patterns vs good nudges.
Key Readings/Resources: Thaler & Sunstein’s Nudge; UIE persuasive design articles.
Optional Specialized UX Topics: Mobile/touch UX, voice/UI, VR/AR fundamentals, IoT/wearables, consumer behavior, and business alignment.

Assignments & Activities: Conduct an accessibility audit, compare ethical and dark-patterned apps, and map a customer journey to a UX touchpoint.

Deliverables: Accessibility audit report, ethics reflection essay.

Module 7: Capstone Project & Portfolio
Week 23–24

Learning Objectives: Integrate mixed methods on a real or simulated product and prepare a job-ready portfolio piece.

Week 23 – Portfolio Case Development: Assemble a UX case study, storytelling, visuals, and research scope.
Key Readings/Resources: NN/g case study guidance; Medium portfolio best practices; Leah Buley, UX Team of One.
Week 24 – Finalization & Presentation: Refine deliverables, present findings, and package the portfolio.
Key Readings/Resources: UIE “Talking to Stakeholders”; portfolio and presentation best practices.

Final Project: Execute a mini research study on a chosen topic, submit a full report with slides, and prepare 2–3 case-study summaries.

Deliverables: Capstone report, polished portfolio excerpts, research presentation slides.
